Some informations on schemes by the central and state govt to support and help farmers:-

   
 1.Krushak Assistance for Livelihood and Income Augmentation (KALIA) Scheme:

    The KALIA scheme is one of the flagship initiatives of the Odisha government aimed at providing financial assistance to farmers for livelihood and income augmentation.
    It offers financial support to small and marginal farmers, landless agricultural households, and vulnerable agricultural households.
    Under this scheme, beneficiaries receive financial assistance for cultivation, purchase of agricultural inputs, and investment in agriculture-related activities.
    Additionally, support is provided for livelihood diversification activities such as animal husbandry, fisheries, and horticulture.
    KALIA aims to improve the economic condition of farmers, reduce distress migration, and ensure sustainable agricultural development.


 2.Odisha Millets Mission:

    The Odisha Millets Mission focuses on promoting the cultivation and consumption of millets in the state.
    Millets are traditional, climate-resilient crops that are rich in nutrients and have low water and input requirements.
    The mission aims to increase millet cultivation through area expansion and productivity enhancement interventions.
    It also includes activities such as capacity building of farmers, promotion of millet-based cropping systems, and creating market linkages for millet producers.

    
3.The Odisha Millets Mission contributes to food security, nutrition improvement, and sustainable agriculture in the state.
    Odisha Integrated Irrigation Project for Climate Resilient Agriculture (OIIP-CRA):

    The OIIP-CRA scheme focuses on enhancing irrigation infrastructure to support climate-resilient agriculture in Odisha.
    It includes the construction and renovation of water harvesting structures, farm ponds, check dams, and irrigation channels.
    The scheme aims to improve water availability for agricultural purposes, especially during dry spells and drought conditions.
    By promoting climate-resilient irrigation practices, OIIP-CRA helps farmers mitigate the impact of climate change on agriculture and improve crop productivity.

   
 4.Odisha State Seed Policy:

    The Odisha State Seed Policy is aimed at ensuring the availability of quality seeds to farmers for crop production.
    It includes guidelines and regulations for seed production, certification, distribution, and marketing.
    The policy emphasizes the promotion of certified seeds of high-yielding and climate-resilient crop varieties.
    It also encourages the development of seed infrastructure, such as seed testing laboratories and seed processing units, to maintain seed quality standards.
    The Odisha State Seed Policy plays a crucial role in enhancing agricultural productivity and supporting sustainable crop production in the state.

   
5.Odisha Fisheries and Aquaculture Development Programme:

    This programme focuses on the development of fisheries and aquaculture sectors in Odisha to enhance fish production and livelihood opportunities for fisherfolk.
    It includes interventions such as pond development, stocking of fingerlings, promotion of aquaculture practices, and establishment of fish seed hatcheries.
    The programme aims to increase fish production, improve fish quality, and boost income generation in rural areas.
    By promoting sustainable fisheries and aquaculture practices, the programme contributes to food security, poverty alleviation, and economic growth in Odisha.
    

    Some Information on Schemes by the Central and State Govt to Support and Help Farmers:
   
1. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN):
 
    PM-KISAN is a central sector scheme launched on 24th February 2019 to supplement financial needs of land holding farmers, subject to exclusions. Under the scheme, financial benefit of Rs. 6000/- per year is transferred in three equal four-monthly installments into the bank accounts of farmers’ families across the country, through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) mode. Till now, Rs. 2.81 lakh crores have been transferred through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to more than 11 crores beneficiaries (Farmers) through various instalments.
    
   
 2. Pradhan Mantri Kisan MaanDhan Yojana (PM-KMY):

    Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojna (PMKMY) is a central sector scheme launched on 12th September 2019 to provide security to the most vulnerable farmer families. PM-KMY is contributory scheme, small and marginal farmers (SMFs), subject to exclusion criteria, can opt to become member of the scheme by paying monthly subscription to the Pension Fund. Similar, amount will be contributed by the Central Government. The applicants between the age group of 18 to 40 years will have to contribute between Rs. 55 to Rs. 200 per month till they attain the age of 60. PMKMY is taking care of the farmers during their old age and provides Rs. 3,000 monthly pension to the enrolled farmers once they attain 60 years of age, subject to exclusion criteria. Life Insurance Corporation (LIC) is pension fund manager and registration of beneficiaries is done through CSC and State Govts. So far 23.38 lakh farmers have enrolled under the scheme.
    
    
3. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY):

    PMFBY was launched in 2016 in order to provide a simple and affordable crop insurance product to ensure comprehensive risk cover for crops to farmers against all non-preventable natural risks from pre-sowing to post-harvest and to provide adequate claim amount. The scheme is demand driven and available for all farmers. A total of 5549.40 lakh farmer applications were insured under the scheme since 2016-17 and Rs 150589.10 crore has been paid as claim.
    
    
 4. Modified Interest Subvention Scheme (MISS):

    The Interest Subvention Scheme (ISS) provides concessional short term agri-loans to the farmers practicing crop husbandry and other allied activities like animal husbandry, dairying and fisheries. ISS is available to farmers availing short term crop loans up to Rs.3.00 lakh at an interest rate of 7% per annum for one year. Additional 3% subvention is also given to the farmers for prompt and timely repayment of loans thus reducing the effective rate of interest to 4% per annum. The benefit of ISS is also available for post-harvest loans against Negotiable Warehouse Receipts (NWRs) on crop loans for a further period of six months post-harvest to small and marginal farmers having Kisan Credit Cards (KCCs), on occurrence of natural calamities and severe natural calamities. As on 05-01-2024, 465.42 lakh new KCC applications have been sanctioned with a sanctioned credit limit of Rs. 5,69,974 crore as part of the drive.
    
    
 5. Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF):

    In order to address the existing infrastructure gaps and mobilize investment in agriculture infrastructure, Agri Infra Fund was launched under Aatmanirbhar Bharat Package. AIF was introduced with a vision to transform the agriculture infrastructure landscape of the country. The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund is a medium - long term debt financing facility for investment in viable projects for post- harvest management infrastructure and community farming assets through interest subvention and credit guarantee support. The Fund of Rs. 1 lakh crore under the scheme will be disbursed from FY 2020-21 to FY2025-26 and the support under the scheme will be provided for the duration of FY2020-21 to FY2032-33. Under the scheme, Rs. 1 Lakh Crore will be provided by banks and financial institutions as loans with interest subvention of 3% per annum and credit guarantee coverage under CGTMSE for loans up to Rs. 2 Crores. Further, each entity is eligible to get the benefit of the scheme for up to 25 projects located in different LGD codes. Eligible beneficiaries include Farmers, Agri-entrepreneurs, Start-ups, Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACS), Marketing Cooperative Societies, Farmer Producers Organizations(FPOs), Self Help Group (SHG), Joint Liability Groups (JLG), Multipurpose Cooperative Societies, Central/State agency or Local Body sponsored Public Private Partnership Projects, State Agencies, Agricultural Produce Market Committees (Mandis), National & State Federations of Cooperatives, Federations of FPOs (Farmer Produce Organizations) and Federations of Self Help Groups (SHGs). As on 31-12-2023, Rs.33.209 Crores have been sanctioned for 44,912 projects under AIF, out of this total sanctioned amount, Rs 25,504 Crores is covered under scheme benefits. These sanctioned projects have mobilized an investment of Rs 56.471 Crores in agriculture sector.
    
    
 6. Formation & Promotion of New 10,000 FPOs:

    The Government of India launched the Central Sector Scheme (CSS) for “Formation and Promotion of 10,000 Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)” in the year 2020. The scheme has a total budgetary outlay of Rs.6865 crores. Formation & promotion of FPOs are to be done through Implementing Agencies (IAs), which further engage Cluster Based Business Organizations (CBBOs) to form & provide professional handholding support to FPOs for a period of 5 years. FPOs get a financial assistance upto Rs 18.00 lakh per FPO for a period of 03 years. In addition to this, provision has been made for matching equity grant upto Rs. 2,000 per farmer member of FPO with a limit of Rs. 15.00 lakh per FPO and a credit guarantee facility upto Rs. 2 crore of project loan per FPO from eligible lending institution to ensure institutional credit accessibility to FPOs. Suitable provisions have been made for training and skill development of FPOs. Further, FPOs are onboarded on National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) platform which facilitate online trading of their agricultural commodities through transparent price discovery method to enable FPOs to realize better remunerative prices for their produce. As on 31.12.2023, total 7,774 FPOs were registered under the scheme in the country.
    
    
 7. National Beekeeping and Honey Mission (NBHM):

    Keeping in view the importance of beekeeping, a new Central Sector Scheme entitled National Beekeeping & Honey Mission (NBHM) was launched in 2020 under Atma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyan for its implementation in the field for overall promotion and development of scientific beekeeping & to achieve the goal of “Sweet Revolution” in the country. The Mission will be implemented for 3 years. It aims to achieve a goal of “Sweet Revolution” by doubling the income of beekeepers and increasing the number of bee colonies in the country. Under the Mission, thrust has been given to attract youth and entrepreneurs for employment generation through commercial beekeeping ventures. Under this scheme, assistance for beekeeping, purchase of bee colonies, tools and equipment, setting up of nucleus colonies and bee breeders’ colonies, training, capacity building, marketing etc., will be provided. As on 31.12.2023, proposals amounting to Rs. 393.25 Crores were received from the states.
    
    
 8. Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay SanraksHan Abhiyan (PM-AASHA):

    PM-AASHA is an umbrella scheme that aims at ensuring remunerative prices to farmers for their produce as announced by the Government. The three components of PM-AASHA are: (i) Price Support Scheme (PSS), (ii) Price Deficiency Payment Scheme (PDPS) and (iii) Pilot of Private Procurement & Stockist Scheme (PPPS). Under the Price Support Scheme (PSS), physical procurement of pulses and oilseeds is done by Central Nodal Agencies with proactive role of States/UTs. As on 31.12.2023, 80,793.35 MT pulses were procured under the scheme through agencies like FCI, NAFED, SFAC and others. Under PDPS, the difference between the MSP and the selling/modal price is directly transferred to the farmer’s account. Under this scheme, Rs 121.97 crore has been transferred to 1.21 Lakh farmers during Rabi 2020-21 crop season. Under PPPS, participation of private sector in procurement operation is tested in selected pilot districts in consultation with the States/UTs. As on 31.12.2023, 39.11 Lakh MT pulses were procured under the scheme.
    
    
 9. Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme:

    The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) scheme was introduced in 1998 to provide timely and adequate credit support to farmers for their cultivation needs. The scheme aims to meet the comprehensive credit requirements of farmers under a single window. KCC provides credit for crop production, allied agricultural activities, and consumption requirements. The card also covers post-harvest expenses, produce marketing, and working capital for maintenance of assets. Additionally, it provides a flexible repayment option to farmers. As of now, 465.42 lakh new KCC applications have been sanctioned with a sanctioned credit limit of Rs. 5,69,974 crore as part of the drive.

    
10. Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY):

    The Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) is a special additional central assistance scheme launched in 2007 to incentivize states to enhance public investment in agriculture and allied sectors. The scheme promotes holistic development of agriculture by supporting projects related to crop diversification, infrastructure development, value addition, and market linkage. RKVY also aims to address regional disparities in agricultural development and increase agricultural productivity and income of farmers. The scheme has been instrumental in catalyzing agricultural growth and modernization across states.

    
 11. Soil Health Card (SHC) Scheme:

     The Soil Health Card (SHC) scheme was launched in 2015 to assess the nutrient status of soil and provide customized recommendations to farmers for improving soil health and fertility. The scheme aims to promote balanced and judicious use of fertilizers based on soil nutrient status, thereby enhancing crop productivity and reducing input costs. SHCs provide information on soil health indicators that can be assessed without the aid of technical equipment, facilitating better decision-making by farmers. The scheme also contributes to the government's goal of promoting sustainable agriculture and environmental conservation.

     
12. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY):

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) was launched in 2016 to provide comprehensive crop insurance coverage to farmers against yield losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. The scheme aims to ensure financial stability to farmers by compensating them for their crop losses and enabling them to recover from adverse events. PMFBY is a demand-driven scheme available to all farmers, covering pre-sowing to post-harvest risks. Since its inception, PMFBY has insured millions of farmer applications and disbursed significant amounts as claim payments, providing crucial risk mitigation and support to farmers across the country.


13. Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF):

The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) was launched under the Aatmanirbhar Bharat Package to address infrastructure gaps and mobilize investment in agriculture infrastructure. AIF provides medium to long-term debt financing for investment in post-harvest management infrastructure and community farming assets. The fund aims to transform the agriculture infrastructure landscape by supporting viable projects through interest subvention and credit guarantee support. AIF disburses funds through banks and financial institutions, with a focus on benefiting farmers, agri-entrepreneurs, and agricultural organizations. The scheme has already sanctioned a significant amount for various projects, mobilizing substantial investment in the agriculture sector.


14. Sub-Mission on Agriculture Mechanization (SMAM):

The Sub-Mission on Agriculture Mechanization (SMAM) was initiated in 2014 to accelerate agricultural mechanization in India. The scheme aims to increase the reach of farm mechanization to small and marginal farmers, promote custom hiring centers, create hubs for hi-tech farm equipment, and provide capacity building and awareness among stakeholders. SMAM has been instrumental in distributing agricultural machinery and equipment to farmers, establishing custom hiring centers, and promoting the adoption of modern farming technologies. The scheme contributes to enhancing agricultural productivity, reducing drudgery, and improving the livelihoods of farmers.


15. Digital Agriculture:

Digital Agriculture is a comprehensive initiative aimed at leveraging digital technologies to transform the agricultural sector. The scheme focuses on developing digital public infrastructure for agriculture, providing farmer-centric solutions through information services, improving access to inputs and financial services, enhancing market intelligence, and supporting the growth of Agri-Tech startups. Digital Agriculture aims to empower farmers with timely information and services, enable efficient resource management, and promote inclusive and sustainable agricultural development. The scheme aligns with the government's vision of creating a digitally empowered and self-reliant agricultural ecosystem.


16. Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH):

The Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) is a centrally sponsored scheme launched in 2014-15 to promote holistic growth in the horticulture sector. MIDH covers a wide range of activities including establishment of orchards, rejuvenation of old orchards, protected cultivation, organic farming promotion, and post-harvest management. The scheme aims to enhance productivity, quality, and marketability of horticulture produce while ensuring sustainable development and livelihood improvement for farmers. MIDH has been instrumental in expanding horticulture cultivation, promoting organic practices, and enhancing value addition in the sector.


17. National Bamboo Mission (NBM):

The National Bamboo Mission (NBM) is aimed at comprehensive development of the bamboo sector in India. Implemented in various states through State Bamboo Missions (SBM), NBM focuses on the entire value chain of bamboo, including plantation, primary processing, value addition, and capacity building. The mission aims to harness the potential of bamboo to generate employment, promote sustainable livelihoods, and contribute to economic growth. NBM has supported the establishment of bamboo nurseries, plantations, processing units, and capacity building initiatives, thereby facilitating the growth of the bamboo sector.


18. Integrated Scheme for Agriculture Marketing (ISAM):

The Integrated Scheme for Agriculture Marketing (ISAM) supports state governments in modernizing agricultural markets and enhancing market access for farmers. The scheme includes initiatives for creating and improving market infrastructure, capacity building, and market information dissemination. A key component of ISAM is the National Agriculture Market (e-NAM), an electronic trading platform that integrates agricultural produce markets across the country. e-NAM facilitates transparent and efficient trading of agricultural commodities, benefiting both farmers and buyers. ISAM has played a crucial role in strengthening agricultural marketing infrastructure and promoting market-led agriculture.


19. Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER):

MOVCDNER aims to promote organic farming and develop organic value chains in the North Eastern Region (NER) of India. The mission focuses on creating certified organic production clusters, supporting organic input production, establishing marketing infrastructure, and capacity building. By promoting organic farming practices, MOVCDNER aims to enhance soil health, conserve biodiversity, and improve farmer incomes in the NER. The mission has made significant progress in establishing organic clusters, supporting farmer producer organizations (FPOs), and building market linkages for organic produce.


20. Sub-Mission on Agriculture Extension (SMAE):

The Sub-Mission on Agriculture Extension (SMAE) aims to strengthen agricultural extension services and promote farmer-centric technology dissemination. The scheme focuses on institutional reforms, digital initiatives, and capacity building activities to enhance the effectiveness of extension services. SMAE supports initiatives such as Agricultural Technology Management Agency (ATMA), digital platforms for extension services, and decentralized extension mechanisms. By empowering farmers with timely information and advisory services, SMAE contributes to improving agricultural productivity, sustainability, and livelihoods.

These schemes represent the government's concerted efforts to address various challenges faced by the agricultural sector and promote its sustainable development. Through targeted interventions, investments in infrastructure, adoption of technology, and capacity building initiatives, these schemes aim to empower farmers, enhance productivity, and ensure food and livelihood security for all.
